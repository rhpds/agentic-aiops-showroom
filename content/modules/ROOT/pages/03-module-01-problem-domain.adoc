== Who you are

You are a senior SRE at **Meridian Financial**, a financial services firm running hundreds of Ansible Automation Platform jobs per week across hybrid VM and OpenShift infrastructure.

Overnight job failures are piling up, compliance deadlines are looming, and the on-call team is stretched thin.
The gap between a failure occurring and a human investigating it is where MTTR accumulates — and where an AI-powered assistant can add immediate value.

Your mission in this workshop: build and evolve an AI-driven incident response assistant that can triage, investigate, and help resolve infrastructure failures — so your team can focus on what matters.

== Connect to OpenShift

Click the **Terminal** tab on the right side of your screen. You'll see two terminal windows.

In either terminal, paste the following command to log in to your OpenShift cluster and switch to your working namespace:

[source,bash,role="execute",subs=attributes+]
----
oc login --insecure-skip-tls-verify \
  -u {user} \
  -p {password} \
  {openshift_api_url} \
  --namespace user-{guid}-agentic
----

You should see:

----
Login successful.
Using project "user-{guid}-agentic".
----

This namespace contains all the services your AI assistant will interact with: Athena, Kira, Rocket.Chat, and more. You can verify with:

[source,bash]
----
oc get pods
----

== Your lab environment

The tab bar across the top of the right panel gives you access to every console in the lab. Let's walk through each one.

=== AAP2 — Ansible Automation Platform

Click the **AAP2** tab and log in with `{user}` / `{password}`.

This is where automation jobs run — and fail. Your AI assistant will receive webhooks from AAP2 whenever a job fails.

Explore these two screens:

* **Job Templates** (left sidebar) — you'll see 9 numbered job templates (`01 Ping RHEL VM` through `09 System Health Check`). These are the automation tasks that Meridian Financial runs against their infrastructure. Some will succeed, some will fail in ways your agent needs to diagnose.

* **Jobs** (left sidebar) — this shows the history of job runs. You may already see a failed job here from the environment setup. This is the data your agent will analyze.

image::lab-and-aap2-01.png[AAP2 console showing job templates and recent jobs,width=100%]

=== Kira — Trouble Ticketing

Click the **Kira** tab and log in with `{user}` / `{password}`.

Kira is the ticketing system where your AI assistant creates structured incident tickets. Think of it as a purpose-built Jira for infrastructure operations.

You should see the **Dashboard** with:

* An open ticket count (at least 1 from the seed job)
* A "Recent Tickets" list showing the ticket title, area, risk, and confidence
* Charts showing ticket distribution by area and risk

Click into the ticket to see the full analysis — this was created automatically by the Athena AI agent that's already running in your namespace.

Click the **Rocket.Chat** tab and log in with `{user}` / `{password}`.

Navigate to the **#support** channel in the left sidebar. You should see a notification from the `aiops` bot matching the ticket you saw in Kira — including the failure summary, risk level, and a direct link to the ticket.

== What's already running

Before you write a single line of code, there's an AI agent already working in your namespace.

**Athena** is an agentic AIOps service that:

1. Listens for failed AAP2 jobs via webhook
2. Analyzes the failure using specialized SRE sub-agents (one each for Ansible, Linux, OpenShift, and Networking)
3. Creates a structured ticket in Kira with root cause analysis, confidence score, and recommended action
4. Posts a notification to the Rocket.Chat `#support` channel

The ticket you saw in Kira and the notification in Rocket.Chat were both created by Athena — automatically, within minutes of a job failing.

In this workshop, you'll explore how Athena works, extend it with new capabilities, and build your own agents using the same patterns.

== The compliance angle

At Meridian Financial, every action taken on a production failure must be traceable. Auditors from the Financial Conduct Authority review change management and incident response records regularly.

An AI agent that acts without logging is a compliance liability. An agent that logs everything it does — and why — becomes a compliance asset. You will build the latter.
